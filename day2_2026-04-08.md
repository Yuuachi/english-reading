## 原文（带注释）

This charismatic canal city is a feast for lovers of the outdoors, but its cosy pubs and secret museums are just as nourishing.

---

Spend any amount of time in Amsterdam and you'll quickly become **acquainted** [熟悉、了解] with the Dutch love of the outdoors. On the finest days, the entire city seems to be made up of cyclists streaming past **gingerbread-worthy** [像姜饼屋一样精致可爱的] **townhouses** [联排城镇住宅]. Theatre performances, markets and music festivals fill the city's parks — which are regarded as outdoor living rooms by many locals — while waterfront terraces **swell with** [涌满] **punters** [（英式口语）普通顾客、闲逛的人] sipping cool beers, the surrounding houses **garlanded** [点缀着] in blooming **hollyhocks** [蜀葵]. Head inside, however, and an entirely different, far cosier city reveals itself. This is the **clandestine** [秘密的、隐秘的] Amsterdam, where locals find **refuge** [庇护所] in low-lit **bruin** cafes (brown cafes) [棕色咖啡馆，因烟熏木质内装得名], historic churches harbour unlikely music venues and grand 17th-century homes conceal the secrets of generations past.

---

### Outdoors

#### Cycle Amsterdam Noord

Cycling is a great way to see the city. **Hire some wheels** [租辆自行车] from Yellow Bike and **download** [下载] its self-guided Amsterdam Noord tour, taking in NDSM Wharf: an old **shipyard** [造船厂] on the banks of the IJ that's been turned into a cultural hub. When the shipyard first closed, it was occupied by artists, and it's still a great place to see street art. **Pause for** [停下来去做某事] a drink at Pllek, a cafe, bar and restaurant **whose** huge glitter ball **looks out onto** [朝向、俯瞰] a sandy beach.

#### Go wild swimming

Marineterrein was also originally a shipyard, built in the 17th century and used by the Dutch East India Company. Today, it's home to a long floating **boardwalk** [木板栈道] that allows access to peaceful, **tree-framed** [被树木环绕的] waters where you can swim and take in views of the National Maritime Museum. The water quality is regularly tested, though there's standup **paddleboard** [桨板] rental if you don't fancy a plunge.

#### Explore a tulip farm

Holland's **tulip** [郁金香] fields, **striped in paintbox colours** [呈现颜料盒般鲜艳的条纹色彩], bloom from late March to May. The Keukenhof gardens are a popular spot, but for something more **intimate** [小而私密的、非商业化的] head to De Tulperij farm, around 20 miles south west of Amsterdam and run by the Jansze **clan** [家族] for generations. Walk among the fields, pick your own tulips or join a tour with one of the Jansze clan to learn more about the history of tulip **cultivation** [种植、栽培] in the Netherlands.

#### Take a migrant canal cruise

Among Amsterdam's many pleasure boat operators, Rederij Lampedusa is unique. With roots in Syria, Somalia, Eritrea, Egypt and the Netherlands, its crew lead canal tours aboard former migrant vessels. Brightly painted and once abandoned by people **smugglers** [走私者、人贩子], the boats now host journeys **during which** storytellers **illuminate** [揭示、阐明] how centuries of migration have shaped Amsterdam.

---

### Indoors

#### Visit a secret church

Few of Amsterdam's hidden spaces are more spectacular than the Our Lord in the Attic Museum. Stepping through the door of this 17th-century merchant's house, you'll wander narrow passageways connecting living quarters filled with period furniture. The real surprise is on the top floor, which conceals a secret church with space for 150 people. It dates to a time when Catholic worship was banned in public, forcing people to **come up with ways of conducting Mass in private** [想出在私下举行弥撒的方法].

#### Hunker down with a pint

One of Amsterdam's most **gezellig** (heartwarming) [荷兰语，温馨舒适、有人情味] experiences is **hobnobbing** [与人亲切交谈、社交攀谈] with locals at one of the city's traditional bruin cafes (brown cafes), so-named because of their dark, wood-panelled interiors stained by centuries of tobacco smoke. Café Chris is said to be the oldest, opening in 1624 on Bloemstraat in the Jordaan district. It's the kind of place you can imagine Rembrandt going to for an ale.

#### Dine at a pirate radio station

Originally an oil rig, REM Eiland became the home of **pirate radio station** [海盗电台，未经授权的非法广播电台] Radio Noordzee in 1964. **After the metal island was seized and dismantled** [这座金属岛被没收并拆除之后], it was rebuilt in Amsterdam's Houthaven port area. Today, the **space-age** [太空时代感的，极具未来感] structure plays host to a restaurant offering up creative seafood dishes like **langoustine** [挪威龙虾] risotto and sea bass with matcha puree. There's a cocktail bar on the floor above, along with a rooftop bar for **raspberry-hued** [覆盆子色调的，即粉紫红色] sunset views.

#### Catch a show at Paradiso

Hippies first **squatted** [擅自占用废弃建筑] in this disused 19th-century church in the late 1960s, and following several clashes with police, were given permission to turn it into a cultural centre. The 1,500-seater Cosmic Relaxation Center Paradiso (now known as Paradiso) opened in 1968 and has **hosted the likes of** [曾接待过…这样的大牌] Pink Floyd and Nirvana since. Acts set to perform below its stained glass windows in 2026 include Belle and Sebastian.

## 句式积累

### 1. 介词 + which（during which / in which / through which）

> 用于在名词后挂一个从句，表示"在其中/通过它"的关系，比普通定语从句更灵活。

**原文例句**：
> The boats now host journeys **during which** storytellers illuminate how centuries of migration have shaped Amsterdam.

**练习句**：
> Beijing hosted the Olympic Games successfully, **during which** the government chose to strengthen the scope of reform and opening-up.

---

### 2. whose 修饰物（不只修饰人）

> whose可以修饰物体或地点，表示"其…的"。

**原文例句**：
> …a cafe, bar and restaurant **whose** huge glitter ball looks out onto a sandy beach.

**练习句**：
> The Forbidden City is a place **whose** red walls symbolize the power of the emperor.

---

### 3. 分词前置做状语

> 用过去分词或现在分词开头，压缩背景信息，句子更有力。

**原文例句**：
> **Brightly painted and once abandoned** by people smugglers, the boats now host journeys during which storytellers illuminate how centuries of migration have shaped Amsterdam.

**练习句**：
> **Long neglected but now fully restored**, the city has become one of the most popular travel destinations in the world.

## 写作原则（今日总结）

| 原则 | 说明 |
|---|---|
| which means → 动词替换 | symbolize / represent / reflect / indicate，根据语境选，比which means更精准 |
| 定语从句不要超过两层嵌套 | 超过两层读者会跟丢；第二层改用whose或分词压缩 |
| 分词前置多用 | 压缩信息量，句子不臃肿，雅思改卷加分项 |
| becoming → has become | becoming表示"正在变"，has become表示"已经成为"，陈述事实用后者 |
| scope vs range | 政策/改革用scope，物理距离用range |
| travel destination（不是traveling） | travel做形容词修饰destination |
