## 一、原文（含注释）

> Across cultures, early spring is a time to **let loose** `[放开玩、撒野]` and play pranks—but historians can't agree on who invented the custom of April 1 fun.

---

> On April 1 every year, millions of people **relish** `[动词：乐在其中、享受]` in the opportunity to pull harmless pranks and **practical jokes** `[行动式恶作剧，区别于说笑话]`. But how did the holiday emerge?
>
> Though April Fools' Day is widely celebrated throughout the English-speaking world, historians haven't yet **pinned down** `[确定、锁定]` a definitive date or time for its emergence. Though it's clear the holiday has existed in some form since at least the 1500s, **competing theories attribute the holiday to** `[句式：把…归因于]` medieval England or more modern France. Others insist the holiday **is rooted in** `[句式：根源在于]` even older celebrations of spring in ancient Rome and elsewhere. Here's why the holiday remains **a merry mystery** `[有趣的谜]` despite competing theories on its origins.

---

### Why April 1? The top three contenders

> Despite its **murky** `[模糊不清的、说不清楚的]` origins, some textual evidence suggests the holiday could be over 600 years old. Folklorist Stephen Winick **lays out** `[清晰列出]` three compelling contenders: one from England, two from France.
>
> The custom of playing pranks on April 1 could date back to the Middle Ages—and evidence of its emergence may have been documented by English poet Geoffrey Chaucer. In his *Nun's Priest's Tale*, published **circa** `[拉丁文缩写，大约（用于年份）]` 1390, Chaucer tells the tale of a rooster and fox who each attempt to fool one another. The poem refers to the pranks taking place 32 days after the beginning of March—which would place it on April 1. But some scholars believe the text includes **typos** `[错别字、印刷错误]` introduced by a medieval **scribe** `[中世纪抄写员]`, **throwing cold water on** `[泼冷水、否定某说法]` an English origin for the holiday.
>
> The other two theories **hail from** `[来自、起源于]` France, which has celebrated "Poisson d'Avril" (April fish) day on the first day of April for centuries. Today, pranksters attempt to attach a paper fish to a victims' back without them noticing, yelling "April fish!" once the victim realizes they've been fooled. But there's potential textual evidence for the custom in a 1508 poem by French poet and composer Eloy D'Amerval, who referred to an "April fish" in his *Book of Deviltry.* This term may provide evidence for connecting fish with foolish people—or actually refer to a custom or holiday already in existence by the 16th century.
>
> Or perhaps calendar confusion—the third contender for an April 1 origin story—**sparked** `[引发、触发]` the French feast. In 1564, the teenaged French sovereign Charles IX went on a major tour of his kingdom, visiting over a hundred towns and cities. As he traveled, he found that different regions and cities adopted different calendars and feast days. So, he **issued** `[颁布（法令）]` the Edict of Roussillon, a document that declared the French new year would fall on January 1.
>
> **Proponents** `[支持者、倡导者]` of the third theory hold that Charles IX's new French calendar convention—or possibly France's adoption of the **Gregorian** `[格里历（现代公历）的]` calendar in 1582—led to widespread confusion about the date of the new year. "Fools" may have insisted the year begins in April, not January, sparking **lighthearted lampooning** `[轻松的讽刺嘲弄]` and a holiday that then spread across Europe and elsewhere.

---

### Even more ancient spring shenanigans `[shenanigans：恶作剧、胡闹（口语，带宽容语气）]`

> Though all three of these potential origin stories of an April 1 holiday are **contested** `[有争议的、尚无定论的]`, they point to spring as time for good-natured pranks and tricks—and **harken back to** `[追溯到、让人想起更早的…]` fun, far older spring rituals. Though it's unclear if these observations directly inspired the April Fools' holiday, many ancient and modern spring festivals incorporate deception, disguise, and good-natured jokes. Take ancient Rome, where the festival of Hilaria was celebrated after the vernal equinox to mark the emergence of spring. Though its origins are also **obscure** `[不清楚的、难以考证的]`, the oldest existing mentions of the holiday date from the third century C.E. The festival was part of a larger event that **paid tribute to** `[供奉；向…致敬]` the fertility goddess Cybele (also known as Magna Mater).
>
> Described by religious historian Jacob Latham as a **"masked carnival marked by licentious behavior"** `[戴面具的狂欢，以放纵行为为特征]`, the springtime festival gave those who celebrated a chance to **unwind** `[放松、解压]` and celebrate with freedom that crossed class barriers. "Anyone can disguise himself as any character he wants," noted Roman historian Herodian, who added that, "there is no position so important or exclusive that someone cannot disguise himself in that dress and play the fool by concealing his true identity."
>
> Other "foolish" festivals celebrated in spring include Holi, a Hindu spring festival that takes place in March and includes **light-hearted** `[轻松愉快的、没有负担的]` pranks, and Sizdah Bedar, an Iranian festival held 13 days after the vernal equinox (April 2) as part of the festival of Nowruz. It's a day for harmless lies and fun that feels similar to April Fools'…yet is thought to date as far back as 536 B.C.E.

---

> Will we ever figure out why April 1 became April Fools' Day? Probably not. As **folklorist** `[民俗学家]` Alan Dundes wrote in 1988, "More than one hundred years of scholarship has unfortunately added very little to our knowledge and understanding of this curious custom."
>
> What is clear is the powerful draw of spring for those looking to **slough off** `[甩掉、摆脱]` the **doldrums** `[低沉沉闷的状态]` of winter and have some light-hearted fun with one another. So if you're an April fish, an **acolyte** `[信徒、追随者]` of Magna Mater or just an April Fool, enjoy this year's chance to prank and make merry—and know you're participating in a tradition that's very old indeed.

## 四、今日句式练习（写完发给我批改）

### 句式 1：competing theories attribute A to B
> 把某事归因于某来源，雅思写作高频结构

**原文**：
> Competing theories attribute the holiday to medieval England or more modern France.

**你写的**（已批改）：
> ~~Cometing theories attribute the economy decay to the war between United States and Iran.~~  
> ✅ Competing theories attribute **economic** decay to the war between **the** United States and Iran.

**注意**：economy（名词）修饰名词时要变形容词 economic；国名前加 the；内容上"美伊战争"略牵强，换成下面这样更自然：
> Competing theories attribute the decline of the Roman Empire to military overexpansion and political corruption.

> 💡 **Tips：想表达"美伊冲突引发全球经济衰退"怎么说？**
>
> ❌ the war / economy decay  
> ✅ Many analysts attribute **the risk of a global economic downturn** to **the escalating military conflict between the United States and Iran.**
>
> | 词 | 意思 | 备注 |
> |---|---|---|
> | **downturn** | 经济下行、衰退 | 比 decay 更常用，decay 偏"腐朽败落" |
> | **recession** | 经济衰退（更正式）| 通常指连续两季度负增长 |
> | **escalating** | 不断升级的 | 描述冲突升级的高频词 |
> | **military confrontation** | 军事对抗 | 美伊现状比 war 更准确 |
> | **armed conflict** | 武装冲突 | 有实际交火但未正式宣战时用 |
> | **sanction** | 制裁 | 经济层面，禁止贸易/冻结资产 |
>
> **war** 在英语里通常指正式宣战的战争，"这边丢炸弹那边射导弹"这种在英语里叫 armed conflict 或 military confrontation，用 war 不是不行，只是严格来讲偏重了一点。

---

### 句式 2：Though + 让步从句，… + 转折主句
> 承认一面，转折另一面，雅思最实用连贯结构之一

**原文**：
> Though it's clear the holiday has existed in some form since at least the 1500s, competing theories attribute it to different origins.

**你写的**（已批改）：
> ~~Though I have not prepared the exam, still choose to losing two hours for exam.~~  
> ✅ Though I have not prepared **for** the exam, **I** still chose to **waste** two hours **on it**.

**注意**：prepare 后加介词 for；主句不能省主语 I；"浪费时间"用 waste（不用 lose）；spend/waste + 时间 + on sth.

---

### 句式 3：harken back to / be rooted in
> 描述事物有更深层历史渊源，比 "come from" 高级很多

**原文**：
> These spring festivals harken back to fun, far older spring rituals.  
> Others insist the holiday is rooted in even older celebrations of spring in ancient Rome.

**你写的**（已批改）：
> ~~The collision bewteen Iran and the United States harken back to the 1979 Iran hostage crisis.~~  
> ✅ The conflict between Iran and the United States **harkens** back to the 1979 Iran hostage crisis.

> ✅ The conflict between Iran and the United States was rooted in the 1979 Iran hostage crisis. （完全正确）

**注意**：主语单数，动词加 s → harkens；collision（物理碰撞）换成 conflict（冲突）更准确。
